I am a PhD student in **Materials Science & Engineering** at **Johns Hopkins University**, where I build machine learning and high-performance computing tools that accelerate the discovery and design of new materials.

My research sits at the intersection of **first-principles simulation (DFT)**, **machine-learning interatomic potentials and graph neural networks**, and **agentic AI**. I work on a range of problems: predicting electronic properties across millions of crystal structures, screening and designing battery cathode materials, learning catalytic adsorption energies, and building [AtomGPT](https://atomgpt.org)-powered agents that let researchers run materials-discovery workflows in natural language. I also care about making these methods fast and reproducible at scale, from single GPUs to multi-GPU Blackwell systems.

Before Hopkins, I earned an M.S. in Chemical Engineering from **Columbia University** and a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from **Penn State**, with prior research in machine-learning potentials for battery materials and DFT studies of catalytic metal oxides. I also helped organize the **2025 Agentic AI for Science Hackathon** at Johns Hopkins.

I am actively looking for **internships and research opportunities** in computational materials science, ML for science, and scientific AI. Feel free to [reach out](mailto:jlee859@jh.edu) — or grab my [CV]({{ '/cv/' | relative_url }}).
